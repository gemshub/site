    GEMS: Gibbs Energy Minimization Software for Geochemical Modeling

    GEMS is a Gibbs Energy Minimization program package for thermodynamic modeling of heterogeneous (geo)chemical systems, especially those involving metastability, kinetics, and dispersity of mineral phases, solid solution - aqueous solution equilibria, and adsorption/ion exchange.

    GEMS provides a versatile framework for modeling complex chemical systems. However, its applicability is inherently limited by the availability and reliability of thermodynamic properties of chemical species or the models required to calculate these properties under the specific conditions of interest, such as temperature, pressure, or compositional range.

    GEM software is distributed "as is" in its present state of development with two purposes:

    • to promote the GEM method and software into the academic research community;
    • to gather the users feedback, vital for making the software more functional and reliable.

    GEM Software is always under development (as indicated in the Newsticker). According to Google Scholar, it has been cited in more than 1500 publications.
